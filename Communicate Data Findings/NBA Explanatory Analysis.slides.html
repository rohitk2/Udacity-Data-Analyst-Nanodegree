NBA Team Performance (1990-2019)¶

by Rohit Kumar¶

Investigation Overview¶

The goal of the investigation is to take certain overall season statistics and analyze team performances over the last 30 years by asking questions. Which teams performed the best in each decade? Which factors are important with winner percentage? How did each team perform in the last 30 years?

Dataset Overview¶

This is a dataset I pulled from an NBA API. Here is the source code: https://github.com/swar/nba_api

Each entry is the overall performance of a team in the last 30 years. The statistics go from 1990-2019 seasons. There are 906 obsevations in the dataset with 17 features. Most variables are numeric in nature, but the variables there are 2 qualitative variables (TEAM_NAME and NBA_FINALS_APPEARANCE).

Which statistics are important and which statistics are not important when it comes to winning?¶

I will be measuring correlation through the Pearson's Correlation coefficient.

  • Less than 0.2: weak correlation
  • (0.2-0.4): moderate correlation
  • 0.4+: strong correlation

This is the only statistic with a strong correlation with win percentage (field goal percentage).

These are the statistics with moderate (0.2-0.4) correlation. As we can see 3-point field goal and assist can almost be considered strong correlation. Defensive Rebounds, Turnovers, and Points Scored have nice corrrelation above 0.3. Rebounds, Personal Fouls and Blocks also have moderate correlations.

NOTE: Personal Fouls and Turnovers had a negative correlation while all others had a positive one

These are the statistics with weak (<0.2) correlation. As we can see Free Throw Percentage, Offensive Rebounds and Defensive Rebounds have a weak correlation.

NOTE: Offensive Rebounds had a negative correlation while others didn't. This surprised me, but my theory is that teams have to miss shots to have a lot of high Offensive Rebounds. This can be tested but for the purpose of this presentation I did not do that.

Which teams have NBA Finals appearances in the last 30 years and how many?¶

As we can see Lakers, Bulls and Spurs have the most rings with 6, 6 and 5 respectively. There are 17 other teams who have made a finals appearance atleast once (8 of which have atleast one ring).

Summary & Conclusion¶

  • statistics with strong correlation with win percentage: field goal percentage
  • statistics with moderate correlation with win percentage: defensive rebounds, turnovers, points scored, rebounds, personal fouls, and blocks
  • statistics with weak correlation with win percentage: Free Throw percentage, offensive rebounds and defensive rebounds
  • Lakers, Bulls and Spurs were the main dominators in last 30 years with 6 rings, 6 rings and 5 rings respectively.
  • 17 other teams besides those 3 made finals (8 of which won atleast 1 time)
  • bulls dominated in the 90's with 6 rings
  • Lakers and Spurs dominated in early 2000s with 4 and 3 rings respectively
  • the last decade had the most turnover, with 7 different teams winning titles (3 rings won by Warriors)
  • teams with the highest consistent win percentage by decade were: 
      - 90s: Trail Blazers, Suns, Bulls, Thunder, and Jazz
  - early 2000s: Pistons, Suns, Lakers, Mavericks, and Spurs
  - last decade: Heat, Warrior, Thunders, Clippers and Spurs
  • some surprising NBA finals winners were 2005 Heat and 1994 Rockets
  • some surprise NBA Conference Winners were 1998 Knicks, 2002 Nets, 2019 Heat, 2009 Celtics, 2001 Nets and the Cavaliers (2006, 2014, 2016, and 2017)
